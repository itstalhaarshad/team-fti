The problem

Grading is the part of teaching that scales worst.

6 hrs

Pakistan's ~2.5 million teachers lose one of the world's longest workweeks to this — and no tool lets them hand off the grunt work without giving up control of the mark.
The insight
The bottleneck isn't reading the handwriting — it's the judgment.
So we skip brittle OCR pipelines. A vision model reads the handwriting AND applies the teacher's own rubric in a single pass.
The AI advises — the teacher stays the final authority on every mark.

Why it's different

Two promises a tired human can't keep.

⚖️ Consistency ledger
A precedent store so the same answer earns the same mark across all 30 students. Student #1 and student #30 are graded by the same standard — that's the core promise, not a detail.
🔍 Full transparency
Every mark carries the AI's reasoning. No black-box scores — the teacher sees why, edits anything, and finalizes.
Built as a 3-agent examiner panel — Rubric Architect → Grader → Summarizer over shared memory. Not one mega-prompt.
Why now

Open field, real buyer.

No local player grades handwritten descriptive answers with AI. Today's systems just route scanned scripts to human markers — proof the workflow is accepted, but the grading itself is still manual. Millions of teachers, a clear institutional buyer.
